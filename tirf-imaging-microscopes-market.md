<p><p>Total Internal Reflection Fluorescence (TIRF) imaging microscopes utilize distinct market types, primarily classified into prism type and objective lens type. The prism type employs an external prism to achieve total internal reflection, generating an evanescent field for surface imaging. In contrast, the objective lens type integrates the TIRF mechanism within the microscope's objective lens, allowing for more versatility and ease of use. Both types cater to specific research needs, enhancing fluorescence visualization of cellular structures and interactions at the nanoscale.</p></p>
