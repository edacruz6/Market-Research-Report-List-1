<p><p>The acellular dermal matrices market comprises four primary types: human, porcine, bovine, and others. Human-derived matrices offer biocompatibility and lower rejection risk, commonly used in reconstructive surgeries. Porcine matrices, sourced from pigs, provide effective wound healing properties and are frequently utilized in burns and surgical wounds. Bovine matrices, derived from cows, are valued for their structural support and versatility in various applications. The "others" category encompasses materials from alternative species or synthetic sources, expanding options in wound treatment and reconstruction.</p></p>
