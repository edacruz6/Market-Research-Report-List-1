<p><p>The automotive seat self-lubricated bearing market includes two primary types: two-layer composite self-lubricating bearings and three-layer composite self-lubricating bearings. Two-layer bearings consist of a solid polymer matrix reinforced with a backing material, providing enhanced wear resistance and reduced friction. In contrast, three-layer bearings incorporate an additional layer, often a lubricant, that further minimizes friction and enhances performance in dynamic conditions. Both types are designed to improve durability and reduce maintenance in automotive seat mechanisms.</p></p>
