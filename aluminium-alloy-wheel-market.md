<p><p>An aluminium alloy wheel is a type of wheel made from a blend of aluminium and other metals, such as magnesium or nickel. These wheels are widely used in the automotive industry due to their lightweight, durability, and aesthetic appeal. They offer better heat dissipation, improved fuel efficiency, and enhanced performance compared to traditional steel wheels. Aluminium alloy wheels are commonly found in passenger cars, commercial vehicles, and high-performance vehicles.</p><p>The Aluminium Alloy Wheel Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing demand for lightweight and fuel-efficient vehicles. The market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 14% during the forecast period. Additionally, stringent government regulations on vehicle emissions are pushing automakers to adopt lightweight materials, further boosting the demand for aluminium alloy wheels.</p><p>Recent trends in the market include the adoption of advanced casting techniques, such as low-pressure casting and flow-forming, to produce stronger and lighter wheels. Customization and design innovation are also gaining traction, with consumers seeking unique and stylish wheel designs. The shift towards electric vehicles (EVs) is another key trend, as aluminium alloy wheels contribute to reducing the overall weight of EVs, enhancing their range and efficiency. Overall, the market is poised for robust growth, driven by technological advancements and evolving consumer preferences.</p></p>

The **Other** category includes hybrid or advanced manufacturing techniques, combining benefits of casting and forging, or innovative processes for niche applications, catering to specific performance or aesthetic demands.</p></p>
