<p><ul><li>Synthetic Automotive Elastomers</li><li>Thermoplastic Automotive Elastomers</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive elastomers market comprises primarily two types: synthetic automotive elastomers and thermoplastic automotive elastomers. Synthetic automotive elastomers are engineered materials offering flexibility, durability, and resistance to weathering, commonly used in seals, gaskets, and vibration dampening components. In contrast, thermoplastic automotive elastomers (TPEs) combine the properties of rubber and plastics, allowing for easy processing and recycling. TPEs are often used in interior and exterior parts, providing design versatility and improved performance in applications such as trim and insulation.</p></p>
