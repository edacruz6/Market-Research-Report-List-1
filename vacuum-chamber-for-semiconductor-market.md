<p><p>A vacuum chamber is a critical component in semiconductor manufacturing, particularly for Physical Vapor Deposition (PVD) and etching processes. In PVD, the vacuum chamber allows the deposition of thin films by evaporating materials in a low-pressure environment, ensuring uniform and high-purity coatings. During etching, the vacuum chamber minimizes contamination and facilitates precise removal of material from the semiconductor surface. Both applications require stringent vacuum conditions to enhance process control, improve yield, and ensure the integrity of the semiconductor devices.</p></p>
