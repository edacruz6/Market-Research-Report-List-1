<p><ul><li>Electrowetting-Based Liquid Lense</li><li>Liquid Crystal Lense</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The liquid lenses market comprises two primary types: electrowetting-based liquid lenses and liquid crystal lenses. Electrowetting-based liquid lenses utilize the principle of electrowetting to manipulate the shape of a liquid interface, allowing for variable focal lengths and rapid focus adjustments, making them suitable for applications in cameras and imaging devices. On the other hand, liquid crystal lenses use liquid crystal technology to control light transmission and refraction, often found in display systems and adaptive optics. Both types enhance optical performance and versatility.</p></p>
