<p><p>The sinus bradycardia drugs market comprises various medication types used to manage abnormally slow heart rates. Atropine increases heart rate by blocking vagal effects on the heart. Isoproterenol acts as a beta-agonist, stimulating heart rate and cardiac output. Aminophylline, a bronchodilator, may enhance cardiac contractility. Ephedrine, a sympathomimetic agent, raises heart rate and blood pressure. Scopolamine, primarily an anticholinergic, can indirectly influence heart rate by reducing parasympathetic tone. Each drug serves specific clinical scenarios in treating sinus bradycardia.</p></p>
