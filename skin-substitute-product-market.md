<p><p>The skin substitute product market is segmented into two primary types based on their sources: animal-derived and human-derived substitutes. Animal source products utilize tissues from pigs, cows, or other animals, often processed to reduce immunogenicity for effective integration into human patients. In contrast, human source substitutes are created from donated human skin or cell cultures, providing a closer biological match for patients and reducing rejection risk. Both types are crucial for wound healing and tissue regeneration in clinical settings.</p></p>
